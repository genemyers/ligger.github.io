When you visit our website, we may send one or more cookies - a small text file containing a string of alphanumeric characters - to your computer that uniquely identifies your browser. You can reset your web browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, the Ligger website may not function properly if the ability to accept cookies is disabled.

When you use specific elements of our services, including instant messaging, our servers sometimes log certain general information that we send whenever a message is sent or received, including time and date stamps and the mobile phone numbers the messages were sent from and to. As well as users being able to voluntarily share their location with other users via our services, Ligger will collect location data, but we anonymous this data. We cannot track any specific users location.

The contents of user submissions that have been delivered by Ligger's services are not copied, kept or archived by Ligger in the normal course of business. We do not store any of your pictures, videos, messages or user submissions on our servers if they are successfully delivered to the intended recipient, and we keep them for no longer than 30 days if they remain undelivered, without your express permission.

The contents of any delivered messages are not kept or retained by Ligger — the only records of the content of any delivered messages reside directly on the sender's and recipient's mobile devices and which may be deleted by the users.

Notwithstanding the above, Ligger may retain date and time stamp information associated with successfully delivered messages and the mobile phone numbers involved in the messages, as well as any other information which Ligger is legally compelled to collect. Files that are sent through Ligger's services will reside on our servers after delivery for a short period of time, but are deleted and stripped of any personally identifiable information within a short period of time in accordance with our general retention policies.
